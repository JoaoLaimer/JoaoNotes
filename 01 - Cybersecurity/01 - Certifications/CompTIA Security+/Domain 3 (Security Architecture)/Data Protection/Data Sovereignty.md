#### Geographical Consideration
The geographic location of data storage and processing can significantly impact businesses.
#### General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) 
Has stringent rules for data protection and grants individuals strong rights over their personal datas.
GDPR mandates that personal data can only be collected and processed with the individual's informed consent, which means the purpose for data collection must be clearly communicated in plain language.

China and Russia have strict data sovereignty laws that require companies to store and process data within their national borders.
#### California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
is a state legislation that provides comprehensive data protection rights to consumers, much like the GDPR. It's considered "horizontal" as it applies across sectors
#### Federal Information Security Management Act (FISMA)
The U.S. Congress created FISMA to cover the IT systems of federal agencies. Specifically, FISMA stipulates that federal government agencies create an information security program.
#### Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act
A legislation that affects the finance sector (is more of a "vertical" regulation as it targets a specific sector). The GLBA restricts the sharing of information with third-party organizations.
