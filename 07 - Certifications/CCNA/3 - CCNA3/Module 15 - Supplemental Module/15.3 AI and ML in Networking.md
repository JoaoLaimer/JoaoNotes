# 15.3.1 The Importance of Explainable AI
In networking, Artificial Intelligence (AI) is becoming a key tool for automating and improving network operations. However, using AI in networking introduces an important difference between two types of AI systems: "black box" AI and Explainable AI (XAI).

**Black Box AI** models operate without showing their internal workings or logic to the user. AlphaZero is an example of this; it makes decisions that are not clear to the user.

**Explainable AI (XAI)** aims to make AI processes and decisions clear and understandable to humans. It ensures transparency and trust in AI decisions, allowing professionals to understand and validate the AI's reasoning.

# 15.3.4 AI in Network Optimization
**Traffic Analysis and Management**

AI plays a key role in traffic analysis and management. By examining traffic patterns and predicting congestion, AI can optimize routing, allocate bandwidth efficiently, and maximize network capability. It reduces traffic congestion and latency by rerouting data through less congested paths, crucial for latency-sensitive applications like voice over IP (VoIP) and real-time streaming. This ensures smooth network operations and high performance for users.

**Enhancing Reliability**

AI boosts network reliability by continuously monitoring for potential failures and addressing them proactively. Predictive analytics help identify failing components, update configurations to avoid vulnerabilities, and maintain high network performance under various conditions.

# 15.3.5 AI in Network Security
AI plays a crucial role in network security by using advanced machine learning models to protect against cyber threats. By learning from network data and user behavior, AI can identify potential security incidents, providing a dynamic defense that adapts to evolving threats.

# 15.3.6 Generative AI Activities for Network Configuration and Troubleshooting

When interacting with generative AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini, how you phrase your questions affects the quality of the responses. This is because these models process and understand language based on how questions are asked. Using precise and clear language narrows down the query, helping the model understand what information you need. Vague or ambiguous questions can lead to broader interpretations, resulting in less specific answers.

Large language models (LLMs) can also use previous conversations to tailor their responses to your preferences. For example, if you often ask ChatGPT networking questions and prefer Cisco IOS, the model will likely provide answers specific to Cisco IOS when you ask about configuring OSPF. This context-aware approach ensures the information is relevant to your interests and needs.

# 15.3.12 LLMs Have Their Limitations

LLMs like ChatGPT are great at processing and generating text. However, they may not solve complex network problems that need real-time data analysis and predictive modeling.

Cisco's AI/ML systems, integrated into their products, are more effective for these tasks. Cisco's AIOps solutions, designed for network engineering, offer advanced features like predictive maintenance, in-depth anomaly detection, and dynamic traffic management.
