## Avoiding Social Engineering
### Social Engineering
A manipulative tactic used by attackers to trick individuals into bypassing an organization's security procedures.
### Situational Awareness
Being mindful of surroundings, tasks, and the potential consequences of one's actions.
### Operational Security (OPSEC)
Stresses data protection against social engineers for business aspects such as routines, project details, and internal procedures.
OPSEC involves involves staying discreet about work schedule and business delas during casual conversations.
- Foster a culture of security with regular cybersecurity training.
- Educate all employees on recognizing phishing attempts and valuing data privacy.
- Encourage employees to report suspicious activities.
- Conduct practical exercises like simulated phishing attacks.
- Remediate individuals who fall for simulated attacks.
## Policy and Handbooks
### Policy
System of rules that guides decisions and actions to ensure compliance with organizational standards and legal ethics.
### Handbook
Concise booklet offering detailed guidance on organization-specific procedures, guidelines, and best practices for individuals.
Policies and handbooks must adapt to evolving cybersecurity, warranting annual reviews and updates.
- Ensure employees read updated policy/handbook.
- Highlight significant changes in a one-page summmary.
## Remote and Hybrid Work Environments
### Remote Work
A work setup in which the employees work from locations outside a traditional office.
### Hybrid Work
Combination of traditional office settings with remote work options.

1. Emphasize the use secure connections, such as VPNs, for data access.
2. Implement a multi-factor authentication for added layer of security.
3. Educate employees on cybersecurity trends and promote a culture that emphasizes security.
4. Consider using company-issued devices with up-to-date security software.
5. Implement automated backup for data protection.
6. Select secure collaboration tools with end-to-end encryption and compliance.
7. Maintain clear communication between cybersecurity teams and remote employees for security resolutions and audits.
## Creating a Culture of Security
### Organization Change Management (OCM)
Recognizing the human role in security, ensuring staff engagement, and policy adherence.
1. Development Phase
	- Create comprehensive policies.
	- Educate employees about threats
	- Implement clear guidelines for handling sensitive data.
	- Build a foundation for employee empowerment.
2. Execution Phase
	- Training updates
	- Simulated cyberattacks
	- Consistent communication
3. Reporting and Monitoring
	- Initial monitoring after the rollout of a security program.
	- Recurring check-ins to maintain program integrity.
	- Educate employees on reporting suspicious activities.
	- Encourage an environment where reporting is valued.
	- Create feedback loops to use insights for future actions.
